    Mortal Kombat 11 Review

  • Reveal Trailer
  • Gameplay Trailer
  • Story/Launch Trailer

  • Platforms: Xbox One, PS4, PC, Nintendo Switch, Google Stadia
    Developers: NetherRealm Studios
    Publishers: Warner Bros. Interactive Enterttainment
    Release: April 23, 2019
    Rating: M
    Reviewed: December 4, 2019 Xbox One
    Description: Mortal Kombat 11 is the latest installment in the critically acclaimed franchise, providing a deeper and more personalized experience than ever before with an all new Custom Character Variation System that gives players the creative control to customize versions of the entire character roster. Developed by award-winning NetherRealm Studios, Mortal Kombat 11 will introduce a new cinematic story continuing the epic saga that is more than 25 years in the making. All-New Custom Character Variation System: Offers nearly infinite customization options, giving players more control and providing a deeper and more personalized experience than ever before. Players can customize their fighters with a variety of Skins, Gear, Special Abilities, Intro and Victory Cinemas, Taunts and Brutalities that can be earned via gameplay. All-New Story Mode: Continue the epic saga through a new cinematic story that is more than 25 years in the making. Players will take on the role of a variety of past and present characters in a time-bending new narrative that pits Raiden against Kronika, the Keeper of Time who created existence at the dawn of history. New & Returning Fan-Favorite Characters: Choose from a roster of fighters with unique abilities and Fatalities, with new characters, such as Geras, a powerful and loyal servant of Kronika who can manipulate time, along with fan-favorite characters, including, Baraka, Raiden, Skarlet, Scorpion, Sonya Blade and Sub-Zero and more. More Modes, Challenges & Rewards: Packed to the brim with multiple modes, including the Towers of Time, which builds upon NetherRealms's past games, allowing players to test their skills through various challenges, providing more ways than ever to continue the Mortal Kombat 11 experience.

    Gameplay: The best aspect of this game is the gameplay. Whether you played any previous Mortal Kombat iterations or not, this game will entice any fighting game enthusiast. Not to mention, the learning curve in this game is almost non-existent so beginners will also be able to enjoy this game. Personally, I have played Mortal Kombat (2011) and Mortal Kombat X and I have to say that Mortal Kombat 11 has improved drastically in the gameplay department. Objectives in fighting games are simple: defeat your opponent. In Mortal Kombat 11, not only can you defeat your opponent, but you can finish them off as well in a variety of unique ways. These include Fatalities that occur at the end of the battle that requires the player to enter a specific combination of button inputs to begin a sequence where your character proceeds to murder your opponent. Brutalities are essentially different versions of combos or moves that are performed as the last one performed before victory after meeting certain requirements like press down, down, down as you do a grab. Varying between the ways you can finish an opponent never gets old! New to the series are varied character intro and outros which is also refreshing. Another new feature is the re-work of the enhanced special move bar. In Mortal Kombat X, your X-Ray attack cost three bars of a meter that had to be shared to enhance special moves as well. In Mortal Kombat 11, they have changed the X-Ray attack to the Fatal Blow that doesn't cost any bars of your meter and can only be performed when you are below a certain health threshold. This mechanic is great for making a comeback in a losing round or match. The enahnced special move bar has also been changed to include two bars for both offensive and defensive options. If you want to enhance a combo, it uses the offensive bar. If you want to escape a punishing combo, you can do so at the expense of your defensive bar. This allows for a better experience in a fighting game that allow players of all skill base to do well in it and subsequently enjoy the game more now that they can do those high damage combos and win.

    In terms of combos and execution of them, it is fairly easy compared to other fighting games like Marvel vs Capcom. There is no combo string that I couldn't perform without much trouble. However, if you want to do complex combos that string together multiple combos and move cancels, you will have to look those up for their timing and specific execution. Once you do, it shouldn't be impossible to pull them off. Mortal Kombat 11 has also included a new Krushing Blow system that enhances a certain move or combo string to show an attack similar to an X-Ray from Mortal Kombat X and allows players to do more damage and sometimes even perform more combos afterward. Similar to Brutalities, Krushing Blows can only be initiated after completing a certain set of requirements beforehand. The environment can also be used to your advantage like using a weapon that is lying around or a platform to jump across the screen with. Overall, all of these new changesto gameplay mechanics are an overwhelming positive addition to the franchise and a worth a lot of praise.

    However, like all fighting games, the character roster also suffers from character balancing. Some characters are so much better than others that it renders some other characters as unplayable due to an improper balancing. Fortunately, not many characters are too overpowered that no character other than them can defeat them. As long as you learn your character, how they play, how fast they are at blocking, attacking, recovering, and their moves, you should be fine. Just stay away from Liu Kang players! Otherwise, the gameplay is solid in this installment in the franchise.
